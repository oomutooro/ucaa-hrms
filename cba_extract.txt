PART I - PRELIMINARY  
 
ARTICLE 1 - CITATION  
This Agreement shall be called the Collective Bargaining Agreement, 2022.  
 
ARTICLE 2 - APPLICATION OF THE AGREEMENT  
This Agreement shall apply to the Authority, the Union, and all Employees of the Authority.  
 
ARTICLE 3 - ENTRY INTO FORCE OF THE AGREEMENT  
This Agreement shall replace the previous Recognition Agreement executed on 05 February 1996 and 
the Collective Bargaining Agreement executed on 01 July 2013 and shall come into force from the _______ 
day of ___________________ 2022, or on such other date as the Parties shall agree.  
 
ARTICLE 4 - MODIFICATION OF AGREEMENT  
The Authority and the Union may by consent modify, cancel or amend any or all of the Articles to this 
Agreement.

PART II - RECOGNITION OF THE UNION AND NEGOTIATING PROCEDURE 
 
ARTICLE 6 - RECOGNITION OF THE UNION BY THE AUTHORITY  
 
6.1 The Authority accords full recognition to the Union as a properly constituted representative body of 
the interests of the Employees of the Authority - 
 
(a)   
in matters concerning terms and conditions of service;  
 
(b)      in consultations and negotiations on the general principles governing but   not limited to rates of 
pay,  overtime,  hours  of  work,  wages or salaries,  transport  and  transport allowance, housing 
and housing allowance, leave, collection of Union dues, medical,  retirement,  redundancy,  
termination  of  employment,  productivity, improvement and maintenance of efficiency; and  
 
(c)     in other matters as may from time to time be agreed upon by the Parties.  

Page 10 of 80 
 
 
6.2 The Union shall present the names of all the officials of the Union and the positions held by them to the 
Authority whenever elections are carried out in accordance with the Union’s constitution and shall 
advise the Authority about any changes therein as and when they occur.  
 
6.3 The Authority undertakes to extend to the officials or representatives of the Union, such facilities as are 
within its means, which are necessary to enable them to conduct their business effectively.  
 
6.4 The Union guarantees that its members will not engage in activities on behalf of the Union during working 
hours, except with the permission of their superior officers, provided that such permission shall not 
be unreasonably withheld.  
 
6.5 The Parties agree that no employee shall be compelled to become a member of the Union nor shall an 
Employee be penalized on account of his or her membership of the Union.  
 
6.6 The Parties recognize their mutual interest in promoting a good relationship between themselves and 
agree to the desirability of productivity and maintaining goodwill in the industry and will co-operate with 
each other towards achieving this.  
 
6. 7 The Authority recognizes that the Union has a role to play in Disciplinary matters provided that the 
nature of such a role shall be as agreed upon between the Parties. In  addition  to  the  above,  the  
Authority  shall  have  an ad-hoc  disciplinary  committee established in accordance with this 
Agreement, to handle issues of disciplinary nature within the Authority. 
  
6.8 The Authority recognizes that employees who do not wish to be members of the Union and its activities, but 
enjoy benefits negotiated by the Union shall pay a monthly service charge as agreed by both parties but 
does not violate the National laws. The service charge shall be deducted monthly from their emoluments.

SHOP STEWARDS 
6.9. Recognition of Shop Stewards 
 
The Authority agrees to recognize Shop Stewards at its premises to represent employees who are 
members of the Union and to make representations on their behalf, in accordance with the law and the 
provisions of this agreement. 
 
6.10 Rights and Duties of Shop Stewards 
 
The   elected Shop Stewards shall be accorded reasonable access to members as provided for in this 
agreement and undertakes to discuss with them matters relating to the employees of the Authority. The 
Union recognizes that elected Shop Stewards are like any other permanent employees, bound by rules 
and regulations prevailing from time to time in the Authority.  Their rights should be exercised as laid 
down in the Laws of Uganda, the Authority regulations, this Agreement and any other Agreement entered 
into by the two parties to this Agreement. 
 
 
Shop Stewards will bring to the notice of their direct superior(s) in their Constituency, any matter 
concerning a member and will carry out their duties without unreasonably or unnecessarily interfering 
with or disrupting their own or other employees’ work.  
 
 
The Authority agrees that for the purpose of carrying out their duties, Shop Stewards and accredited 
Union officials may meet as per their schedule or whenever need arises.   
 
6.11 Victimization 
 
The Authority undertakes that no Shop Steward shall be victimized as a result of his/her exercising his/her 
right as a Shop Steward in good faith and in pursuance of his/her duties as a Shop Steward and in 
accordance with this Agreement. The Authority will not victimise any employee by virtue of his/her Union 
membership. 
 
6.12 Notice Boards 
 
The Authority shall allow the Union the use of Authority notice boards for the display of notices by the 
Union, provided that such notices shall have been submitted to and approved by the Authority, prior to 
being displayed and the Authority agrees that it shall not unreasonably withhold such consent.

ARTICLE 7 - CENTRAL NEGOTIATING COUNCIL 
7.1 Establishment of the Central Negotiating Council 
a) For efficient conduct of their affairs and in order to maintain good Industrial Relations, the   
Authority and the Union shall establish a CENTRAL NEGOTIATING COUNCIL (CNC) for 
purposes of discussing and settling matters of mutual concern.  
b) CNC shall consist of ten members, each party being represented by five (5) members, or such 
other number as the Parties may from time to time agree.  The quorum of CNC shall be the 
Chairperson, a mandated Union spokesperson from ATGWU and a mandated Management 
spokesperson (DHRA or representative) together with at least two (2) members from either party. 
Other persons, not being members of CNC, may attend as observers by mutual consent of both 
Parties.  
c) An independent Chairperson agreed upon by both Parties shall chair the CNC. CNC shall have a 
Secretary and Deputy Secretary mutually appointed amongst the Union and Management 
representatives.  
d)  Alternate Chairperson 
 
There shall be an alternate Chairperson agreed upon by both parties who shall chair the CNC in absence 
of the chairperson. 
e) CNC shall be the highest negotiating body between the Parties, and shall regulate its procedure 
for Meetings.  
 
7.2 Functions of the Central Negotiating Council 
 
CNC shall act as machinery through which the Authority shall negotiate with the Union, and shall in 
particular discuss and determine any questions affecting employees as shall be referred to it by either 
the Authority or the Union in connection with: 
a)  Terms and Conditions of Service;  
b)  Productivity, improvement and maintenance of efficiency of office and working conditions, re-
organisation and methods of work, including safety and welfare arrangements; or  
c)  Any other matters, of mutual interest to the Parties involving UCAA Employees.

ARTICLE 8 - STAFFING REQUIREMENTS  
8.1 The governing body of the Authority shall be the Board, which shall determine and create staff positions, 
as empowered under the Act Cap 354.  
 
ARTICLE 9 - FILLING OF POSITIONS  
9.1 Positions may be filled through promotions arising out of Performance Appraisal as provided for by Article. 
22 of this Agreement. 
 
9.2 The Board is the competent Authority for determining the appropriate recruitment process for all positions 
in the Authority.  The filling of vacancies in the Authority shall be determined using any of the following 
processes. 
a) Appraisal/Promotion. 
b) Internal Advert. 
c) External Advert 
The Board reserves the right/discretion to determine the appropriate recruitment method as they deem fit. 
 
9.3 Where necessary, recruitment will be undertaken through head hunting. However head hunting shall be 
resorted to in compelling circumstances where special skills are required in the Authority. 
 
9.3 A position that has been internally or externally advertised shall be filled by requisite qualifications stipulated 
in Article 13, however internal applicants shall also be considered for equal or higher to their present 
positions not withstanding that the applicant is willing to take up a lower position.

ARTICLE 11 - APPOINTMENTS 
All appointments by the Authority shall be in writing in form of a Contract of Employment indicating the 
terms and conditions of service of employment.  
 
ARTICLE 12 - CATEGORIES OF EMPLOYMENT  
The following shall be the categories of employment into the service of the Authority: 
 
a) Permanent Employee 
This is an employee who has been hired for a position without a pre-determined time limit. He/she will be 
deemed to become a permanent employee after confirmation in service by the Authority. All permanent staff 
shall be appointed to established positions. 
 
 

Page 15 of 80 
 
b) Contract Employee  
i) Fixed term contractual appointment 
This is where an employee is appointed for a fixed period, this appointment maybe for the purposes 
of filling an established executive positions Exec 1 – 3, any other category as may be determined 
from time to time or any other assignment as may be deemed necessary by the Authority. Such 
position may be fixed from one (1) – four (4) years renewable 
 
ii) Short term contractual appointment 
This refers to the contractual appointment after attaining a mandatory retirement age, in the 
Employment Act or special assignment as determined and shall be for the period of up to one (1) 
year 
 
c) Seconded Employee 
       
This is an employee engaged under an agreement between the Authority and his/her principal employer, 
which the employee shall agree to abide by upon execution. A seconded employee shall fall under the 
chain of command of the Authority through appropriate channels and subject to the General Terms and 
Conditions of Service of the Authority. This category shall be subject to control and discipline of the 
Authority. 
 
d) Casual Employee 
This is an employee engaged on a day-to-day basis and paid a daily wage to handle work whose volume 
or nature would not necessitate permanent employment.  This type of employee shall not work for a 
continuous period exceeding 90 days.  When a suitable vacancy comes up within the authority, a casual 
employee maybe considered for employment subject to meeting hiring requirements or through a 
performance appraisal system. A casual employee who has been laid off may be re- engaged depending 
on the need.  
 
e) Temporary Employee 
This is an employee engaged under a  temporary appointment to handle work in a temporarily vacant 
position due to leave, sickness or temporary indisposition of the substantive employee where there is no 
other suitable in-service employee to act. Management can engage a temporary employee to do additional 

Page 16 of 80 
 
assignments that arise from time to time for a period not exceeding one year unless renewed. A temporary 
employee’s terms of service shall be specified in his/her instruments of appointment. 
 
ARTICLE 13 - GENERAL CONDITIONS RELATING TO APPOINTMENTS 
      13.1 A person shall be eligible for appointment to a position in the Authority where that person – 
a) is eighteen (18) years old and above, except where such recruitment is for apprenticeship or on 
a temporally basis and is permissible under the Employment Act and any other applicable laws;  
b) is not above mandatory retirement age; 
c)  meets the academic and other qualification requirements for the position available;  
d) is certified medically fit by a designated medical facility, with full regard to Schedule 7(HIV Policy) 
of this agreement. However, people with disability and depending on suitability of this person for 
the job shall not be discriminated. Notwithstanding the medical fitness at recruitment, medical 
checks will be done to determine subsequent suitability for the job. In the event that medical 
suitability has been lost, the appropriate decision on the person’s continuous employment will be 
taken by the competent Authority; 
  
e) has never been convicted of an offence involving moral turpitude; 
f) in the case of a non - citizen of Uganda, such person is duly authorized to work in Uganda, and 
satisfies all qualification criteria specified in a), b), c), and d) above;   
 
13.2 Medical fitness requirements for various positions will be notified from time to time by the Director   
responsible for Human Resource and Administration and will determine continued employment as the 
requirements of the industry prescribe. 
  
13.3 Every Employee shall communicate his or her bio-data to the Directorate for Human Resource and 
Administration on first appointment, and whenever changes occur. 
 
13.4 Every Employee shall notify the Directorate for Human Resource and Administration about any 
changes in their official names which may occur as a result of marriage, custom and any other 
changes provided that legal documentary supporting evidence is tendered.

ARTICLE 14 - COMMENCEMENT OF SERVICE  
Service shall be deemed to have commenced on the working day on which an Employee reports for 
duty following appointment as witnessed by immediate supervisor. However, if such employee reports 
on duty in the afternoon, he/she will be regarded as having reported on the following day.  
 
ARTICLE 15 - PROBATION  
15.1 A newly recruited employee will be required to serve a probation period of Six (6) months before 
confirmation in the appointment. However, probation may be for a longer period in positions where a 
trainee is required to undergo ab initio training. 
 
15.2 During the probationary period either party may terminate employment by giving to the other fourteen 
days’ notice, or by payment to the employee of seven days wages in-lieu of notice.  
 
15.3 The Authority may extend the Probation for a further period of not more than Six (6) months. 
 
15.4 In order to assess the performance of a probationer, a system of quarterly appraisal will be adopted. 
 
15.5 No employee shall be required to serve more than one period of probation.  
 
15.6 Without prejudice to the foregoing provisions, the competent Authority may for good cause waive 
Probation or any part thereof.

PART VII - WORKING HOURS, REST INTERVALS, HOLIDAYS AND OVERTIME & EXTRA HOURS 
 
ARTICLE 46 - LENGTH OF WORKING HOURS AND REST INTERVALS 
46.1 The Authority and the Union recognise that the Air transport industry operates twenty-four (24) hours a 
day. The standard working hours shall not be more than forty Eight (48) hours per week for office 
workers (Monday to Friday) and forty eight (48) hours per week for shift workers, spread over five days 
within any seven days, so as to give each employee at least two days of rest over the period. 
 
 

Page 35 of 80 
 
46.2 Rest/meal intervals shall be the subject of local arrangements to suit operating conditions at a particular 
duty point, it being understood that during periods of abnormal pressure, staff may be required to work 
through normal rest, meal intervals. In such circumstances, an alternative rests/meal interval will be 
allowed, if practicable, immediately before or after the time of the normal rest interval. 
 
46.3 All Employees shall work at the time and station notified to them.

ARTICLE 47 - PUBLIC HOLIDAYS 
47.1   Public holidays shall be those gazetted from time to time by the Government of Uganda. 
 
47.2   Such holidays shall be observed by all Employees other than those whose retention on duty is required 
by the Authority for operational reasons. 
  
47.3   The Employees shall be entitled to meals.  
In addition to meals, when an employee works during a public holiday, the Authority shall pay the 
employee an equivalent of Safari Day Allowance as per Authority determined rates.  
 
ARTICLE 48 - OVERTIME  
48.1  Employees in the Levels 1-8 shall be paid overtime, while those from Level 9 and above who are 
nonexempt shall qualify for extra hours’ allowance. In order to qualify for overtime/extra hour’s payment, 
it shall be authorized by the Authority who shall ensure that adequate supervision was provided and 
that over time essential to the best interests of the Authority shall be worked.  
 
48.2 Overtime hours shall be determined by Management in accordance with the law.  
 
48.3 Payment rates of overtime worked shall be determined by the union and Management from time to time.

PART VIII - CONTINUOUS SERVICE  
 
ARTICLE 49 - CONTINUITY OF SERVICE 
49.1 An Employee’s Continuous Service shall not be regarded as broken where an Employee is absent 
from work - 
a)  Due to his or her taking Leave.  
b)  Due to his or her suspension with or without pay;  
c)  Due to having been temporarily laid off by the Authority 
d) During the time of court proceedings up to the time of conviction. However, this does not apply to 
traffic offences.  
e)  In accordance with the Agreement or Contract of Service between the Employer and Employee.  
g)  A series of cumulative consecutive contracts shall be regarded as continuous service.  
 
49.2 Any period of time lapsing in a situation referred to in 49.1 above shall count for purposes of calculating 
length of continuous service.  
PART IX - CESSATION OF SERVICE  
 
ARTICLE 50 - RETIREMENT  
50.1 “Retirement” shall be understood to apply to an Employee who ceases to offer his or her service to the 
Authority upon attaining the age of sixty (60) years or such other age as may be determined from time 
to time by the National laws.  
  
50.2 Upon attainment of the age of sixty (60) years, an Employee shall automatically retire.  
 
ARTICLE 51 - VOLUNTARY RETIREMENT  
An Employee who completes fifteen (15) or more years of continuous service for the Authority may voluntarily 
retire upon attaining the age of fifty-five (55) years and after giving the Authority not less than three (3) 
months written notice of voluntary retirement.  
 

Page 37 of 80 
 
ARTICLE 52 - RETIREMENT ON MEDICAL GROUNDS  
The Authority may require an Employee to compulsorily retire due to medical unfitness or other incapacity 
resulting in failure by the Employee to carry out his or her duties. To determine medical fitness, the Authority 
may refer the employee to a designated medical examiner to examine the Employee and give its 
recommendations. However, the employee may opt to have a medical examiner of his/her choice present at 
this examination. 
 
ARTICLE 53 – DEATH 
Upon the death of an employee, the date of death shall be deemed the effective date of termination. 
 
 ARTICLE 54 - RESIGNATION BY AN EMPLOYEE  
54.1 For avoidance of doubt, an Employee who intends to resign from the service of the Authority, which 
in all intents and purpose amounts to termination by the Employee of his or her Contract of 
Service, will be required to comply with the notice requirement stipulated under Article 51. 
PROVIDED that prior to the resignation, the Employee requests and obtains the Authority’s waiver in 
writing, of part or the entire notice required.  
 
54.2 The Authority and the Employee may also agree to adjust the balance on Annual Leave at the credit of 
the Employee towards the notice period, if circumstances of the case so demand. 
 
ARTICLE 55 - ABSCONDMENT  
a) An employee who absents himself/herself from duty for fourteen (14) consecutive working days without 
permission or communicating to the supervisor with justifiable reasons, shall be assumed to have 
absconded from work and shall have automatically terminated his/her services with the Authority unless 
with justifiable reason and in such circumstances it is the duty of the supervisor to bring the matter to the 
responsible director who will in turn notify the Director Human Resource and Administration who must 
bring the Matter to Management at the next Management sitting. 
 
b) A supervisor who fails to notify the relevant authority about an employee’s abscondment will be subject 
to disciplinary action as per Article 80.2. 
  
 

Page 38 of 80 
 
ARTICLE 56 - TERMINATION OF CONTRACT OF SERVICE  
Termination shall be deemed to take place in the following instances: 
a) Where the Contract of Service is voluntarily ended by the Authority or the Employee, with notice as 
provided for under this Agreement; 
b) Where the Contract of Service, being for a fixed term or task, ends with the expiry of the specified 
term or task and is not renewed within a period of one (1) Month from the date of expiry, the incumbent 
he/she shall be informed in writing; or  
c)  Where the Contract of Service is ended by the Authority for disciplinary reasons, stipulated in Article 80. 
 
ARTICLE 57 - DIVESTITURE, PRIVATISATION, TAKE OVER 
57.1 Where the Authority divests or privatizes or hands over part or the whole Section or Department to another 
employer, the Authority shall treat the affected employees as under Redundancy under Article.61. 
 
57.2 An employee’s contract of service shall not be transferred to the new employer without his/her consent.  
 
57.3 All affected employees by the above Article 57 shall be paid all their due benefits prior to their exit. 
 
ARTICLE 58   CHANGE/TRANSFER OF AUTHORITY/UNION DESIGNATION 
a) In the event of the Union re-registering with the Registrar of Trade Unions under another name or transfers 
its members to another trade union during the term of this agreement, the agreement shall continue to apply 
and remain binding on the successor workers organisation. 
b) In the event of the Authority re-registering under another name or transferring its business or part of it to 
another employer during the term of the Agreement, the agreement shall continue to apply and remain 
binding on the successor employer.  The existing employee shall however retain the option to continue with 
a new employer or to be retired on the existing terms.  Thereafter, the employee shall be free to enter a new 
contract of employment with the incoming employer.

The date of termination shall be deemed to be:

c) The date of retirement. 
d) The date of death.  
 
ARTICLE 61 - REDUNDANCY AS A GROUND FOR TERMINATION BY THE AUTHORITY  
Redundancy shall be a ground for termination of a Contract of Service, with notice and other conditions

b) All allowable allowances up to the date of redundancy. 
c) Where applicable, any Long Service Awards which may have accrued at the time of the Redundancy. 
d) Severance pay at the rate of one (1) month’s pay per completed Year of service; 
e) Service Gratuity as provided for under Article 73 and 
f) Transport Facilitation at the rate of one (1) month’s pay. 
g) Accumulated leave.

PART XI - DISCIPLINARY CODE, RULES AND PENALTIES  
 
ARTICLE 75 - DISCIPLINARY COMMITTEE  
75.1 The Authority shall constitute a Disciplinary Committee to hear allegations of indiscipline and 
violations of this Agreement and to make appropriate recommendations to the Authority.  
 
75.2 The Disciplinary Committee’s composition and chairperson will be determined by the Authority, bearing 
in mind the rules of fairness, natural justice and appropriate seniority of the members, PROVIDED that 
the Directorate responsible for Human Resource and Administration, the Directorate of the Employee 
against whom charges are brought and the Union, are represented. As much as possible, every 
Disciplinary Committee should have a representative from the legal department. 
  
 
 
75.3 The Director Human resource shall ensure that the Employee appears before the disciplinary hearing, 
as soon as it is availed with the relevant details and information; such summon shall be in writing. 
 
ARTICLE 76 - DISCIPLINARY PROCEEDINGS  
76.1 Where an Employee is suspected of having violated this Agreement, the General terms and 
conditions of service or any other Regulations of the Authority, disciplinary procedures will be 
implemented without unnecessary delay, after proper investigations are completed, where necessary.  
 
76.2 Where a decision to implement a disciplinary procedure is taken, the Authority shall at the first 
opportunity - 
a) Inform the Employee, in writing, of the violation(s);  
b) Remind the Employee of his or her right to prepare and submit a written explanation, and to 
appeal any decision;  
c)  Give the Employee reasonable time to prepare a response or defense to the allegations against him 

Page 47 of 80 
 
or her;  
d) Inform the Employee of the form the disciplinary proceedings will take;   
 
ARTICLE 77 - RULES OF DISCIPLINARY HEARINGS  
The following will be the Rules of Procedure for Disciplinary Hearings - 
a) The Chairperson will chair the hearing and direct the proceedings assisted by other members;  
b) The Committee will appoint a Secretary, who will be a representative from the Directorate responsible for 
Human Resources and Administration;  
c) Members of the Disciplinary Committee and the employee shall be given an opportunity to raise any 
concern over conflict of interest at the earliest opportunity, which will be considered and ruled upon by 
the chairperson;  
d) Statement of facts:  The Chairperson will deliver or direct a member of the Committee to read the facts 
constituting the alleged violation(s) to the Employee;  
e) Taking of Plea: After reading the facts, the Chairperson will invite the Employee to plead or raise preliminary 
points/questions to the facts;  
f)  Burden of Proof: The burden of proving the violations will rest on the Authority;  
g)  Standard of Proof: Disciplinary proceedings are civil in nature. Accordingly, the standard of proof is on a 
balance of probabilities;  
h)  Failure to Attend hearing by an Employee: Where an Employee fails, refuses or neglects to turn up for a 
hearing, having received adequate notice of the hearing and without any justifiable reason, the 
Disciplinary Committee will decide whether to proceed with the hearing and consider only the written 
defense submitted by the Employee;  
i) The Hearing:  The proceedings of the Committee are formal, and will be conducted in a the most 
convenient manner that ensures clarity of issues;  
j) The Decision: The Committee will deliberate on the issues in absence of the Employee, and will report 
its recommendations to the Competent Authority;

SCHEDULE 7: HIV/AIDS POLICY 
 
The actual and potential growth and impact of HIV/AIDS on people of productive and economically active 
age poses an enormous global and business challenge. Employees are critical to the success of the aviation 
business. The loss of an employee is not only a personal and family tragedy, but also results in direct and 
indirect loss to the Authority in terms of medical costs, retraining, recruitment and productivity. The Authority 
is committed to the protection and maintenance of its employee’s health and wellbeing within affordable limits. 
 
 
The objective of this HIV/AIDS policy is to minimize, monitor and manage the impact, spread and 
consequences of HIV/AIDS in as far as it affects the employees and the operations of the Authority. 
 
The key elements of this policy are to: 
 
(i) 
Provide health information to all employees on HIV/AIDS, its magnitude, impact and preventative 
measures. 
(ii) 
Clearly define HIV/AIDS policies and procedures taking into account local practices, procedures, 
culture and legislation. 
(iii) 
Implement non-discriminatory policies, procedures and practices in managing individuals who 
have HIV/AIDS. 
(iv) 
Approach individuals who have HIV/AIDS in the same manner as those with any other 
progressive debilitating illness and with all the support this entails. 
 
 
Non-Discriminatory Policies, Procedures and Practices: 
 
Misconceptions and misinformation regarding HIV/AIDS can contribute to discriminatory management 
policies, practices and behaviors resulting in panic and poor employee morale and working relationships 
between employees and those infected with HIV/AIDS.  
 

Page 68 of 80 
 
The Authority therefore recognizes a need to disseminate standard, consistent and factual information on the 
HIV/AIDS disease and to educate our employees to minimize and manage HIV/AIDS infection. 
 
Education Programme and Dissemination of Information: 
The Authority will develop and regularly disseminate information on the magnitude of the disease and 
developments in scientific research, especially those relating to prevention. 
To implement this effectively, the Authority will distribute an HIV/AIDS Staff Handbook, Posters, Fliers and 
other media-based information to all employees and will encourage staff attendance on the “Staying Alive” 
awareness presentation. 
 
All relevant information will be held centrally by the Human Resource Department accessible to all Managers, 
employees, and where possible employees’ families. 
 
Support for preventive programmes: 
 
The Authority will actively support and identify reputable local and international Non Governmental 
Organizations (NGO’s) and medical networks to contribute to our HIV/AIDS prevention and education 
programmes including counseling, awareness education and distribution of free condoms within the Authority 
premises. In addition the Authority will utilize its medical network to educate employees on the risks 
associated with Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STD’s) and encourage affected individuals to seek complete 
treatment. 
 
Managing HIV/AIDS at the workplace 
 
Job Applicants: 
The Authority is an Equal Opportunity Employer and will not use HIV testing when recruiting job candidates. 
The Authority will ensure that job candidates who are known to have HIV/AIDS are treated no differently from 
those who may have another life-threatening and non-contagious disease. Fitness to perform a job will be 
the only criterion that shall apply. 
 
 

Page 69 of 80 
 
HIV Testing and Screening: 
The Authority does not consider HIV testing and screening of employees to be necessary or desirable. 
 
Confidentiality OF HIV/AIDS information: 
 
The Authority considers medical information including HIV/AIDS status confidential, unless disclosure is 
required and only under the following circumstances. 
• The information is legally required 
• The employee’s health is affecting their own and hence Authority’s performance. 
• Long employee absence necessitates management to obtain the employee’s medical status with the 
employee’s written permission. 
In all the above circumstances disclosure on HIV/AIDS status should only be made with the written approval 
of the Human Resource Manager and only after obtaining a written authority from the individual concerned. 
Should it become known in the Authority that an employee is HIV positive then Management should ensure 
the absolute confidentiality of this information and make every effort to protect that person from stigmatization 
and discrimination. 
To ensure maintenance of confidentiality these policy and procedural statements should be implemented and 
communicated to all those who handle personnel information. Any unwanted disclosure by any employee of 
the Authority will be treated as an act of gross misconduct warranting disciplinary action. 
 
Counseling of HIV/AIDS infected employees 
 
The Authority recognizes that HIV/AIDS infected employees are vulnerable to discriminatory policies, 
practices and behaviors, and loss of training, promotion and career opportunities. 
This can cause great distress and alienation. 
The Authority regards any form of discrimination and harassment, directly or indirectly, towards HIV/AIDS 
infected employees as a gross misconduct warranting strict disciplinary action. 
Harassment is any form of verbal or non-verbal behavior that is directed at the infected individual resulting in 
the individual feeling threatened, insecure or distressed. 
 
 

Page 70 of 80 
 
Examples of harassment include: 
• Offensive gestures and facial expressions 
• Derogatory remarks, jokes and ridicule 
• Invasive personal questions. 
• Discriminatory behaviors and practices 
• Unwarranted staring 
• Offensive letters, memos, emails and publication of Patronizing comments and nicknames 
• Making fun of the individual. 
 
Discrimination of HIV/AIDS infected employees will not be condoned. This includes treating those affected 
differently or not applying a professional judgment in making personnel decisions. 
 
Employees’ involvement: 
All employees, or their representatives, and particularly those infected by HIV/AIDS should be involved in the 
formulation and implementation of this policy. 
 
Segregation and Isolation of HIV/AIDS infected employees: 
No employee will be isolated, or segregated, within the Authority’s premises on the grounds of being infected 
with HIV. Where an employee has a contagious disease as a result of HIV infection, they will be treated in 
exactly the same manner as if a non- HIV infected employee had contracted the infection. 
 
HIV/AIDS and first aid: 
The Authority is committed to maintaining a safe working environment for all its employees, customers and 
visitors in the working premises. The Authority will therefore take all reasonable and practical steps to ensure 
that: 
 
• A  representative number of employees in all functional units is trained to provide First Aid in cases 
of emergency including taking appropriate precautions to reduce the risk of transmitting blood borne 
infections, including HIV/AIDS and Hepatitis B. 
• First Aid facilities and particularly all necessary equipment for dealing with spillage of body fluids are 
fully stocked and replenished regularly. This equipment will include disposable gloves and aprons, 

Page 71 of 80 
 
suitable bleaches/disinfectants, disposable toweling and impervious plastic bags for disposal of used 
materials. 
 
Sick Leave and Absence through HIV/AIDS illness 
 
Employees who are prevented from performing their duties due to HIV/AIDS illness will be granted sick leave 
to regain their health. Sick leave is allowed under the terms and conditions in Section 45. 
 
Redeployment and termination of HIV/AIDS infected employees 
 
Where the Authority is advised by a medical authority that an employee is unfit to carry out normal duties, 
and there are no grounds to indicate that improvement is likely in the foreseeable future, then an interview 
must be held with the employee to explore alternative future employment positions including: 
 
• Re-deployment to lighter duties 
• Alternative employment terms and condition including shorter daily or weekly working hours. 
• Termination on capability and medical grounds 
 
In exploring the above alternatives the following guidelines must be observed: 
 
• A qualified medical opinion is obtained before any final decision is taken 
•  Alternatives are discussed and explored gradually with the employee first being advised that their 
absence from work places their employment at risk. 
• A date to terminate is set if there is no return to work 
•  All interviews and actions agreed are confirmed in writing to the employee 
•  Management must ensure compliance with policies and procedures on termination, deployment, 
sick leave, absenteeism and grievance policies and procedures

SCHEDULE 8: HEALTH & SAFETY POLICY 
 
It shall be the duty of the Authority: 
a) To take all reasonable steps to ensure the health and safety of all employees at work and to take all 
necessary steps to implement such a policy. 
b) To promote the safety and well-being of its employees. 
c) To build a positive and healthy work environment. 
d) To ensure that the working environment is free from any hazards. 
e) To provide appropriate information, training and supervision necessary for the safety and health of 
employees. 
f) To provide adequate protective equipment to prevent risk of accidents. 
g) To ensure the health and safety of other people who may be affected by the work activity and 
reasonable steps will be taken by all concerned to ensure that this duty is observed 
 
The employee shall: 
(a) Co-operate with the Authority in all measures to minimize risks to health and safety at work. 
(b) Co-operate with the Authority to ensure that this policy is effective and to offer all necessary 
assistance to ensure health and safety at work of all employees 
(b) Report to the immediate supervisor any situation which he or she believes to present a serious danger 
to his or her life or any other person. 
 
Protective Equipment 
All employees working in hazardous environment that may expose them to injury shall be provided with 
protective wear and equipment. 
Group Personal Accident Cover 
The Authority shall provide a twenty four (24) hour cover against any injury to any of its employee while 
on duty. The benefits of this scheme shall be in line with the provisions of the Workers Compensation 
Act. 
 

Page 73 of 80 
 
Workstations 
 
The workplace must be kept clean and tidy, with rubbish and discarded materials carefully placed in the 
receptacles provided. Proper attention must be paid to hygiene.  
 
All materials must be properly and safely used and when not in use properly and safely secured. Care 
must be taken to ensure that the workplace is not hazardous either to employees or to third parties 
including trespassers. 
 
Access 
 
Means of access to and egress from the workplace must only be used for the purpose for which they are 
provided. No employee must tamper with any means of access to or egress from the workplace. Any 
means of access to or egress from the workplace, which is in a dangerous or unsuitable condition for 
use must be reported by employees to the employee’s immediate supervisor. 
 
Carrying heavy items 
 
Lifting or carrying items such as machinery, heavy bundles of paper or files should be done carefully. 
Employees should ask for assistance if there is any danger of strain. Heavy objects should be lifted in 
the correct manner without bending the back. 
 
Fire 
a) Employees should ensure that they are familiar with the position of the nearest firefighting equipment, 
alarms and emergency exits. They should ensure that they understand how to operate such 
equipment. In particular, employees should use electric appliances with caution and to keep 
flammable materials, in particular paper, away from sources of heat. Employees should report any 
faulty electric cable or flex immediately. Emergency exits, stairs, corridors and doorways should not 
be abstracted. Fire doors should always be kept closed. 
 

Page 74 of 80 
 
b) Employees should be fully conversant with the fire drill and be aware of the nearest fire exit and 
assembly points. Employees should leave the building quickly and in an orderly fashion following the 
correct route. 
 
Electrical Equipment 
a)  Any equipment in a dangerous or worn-out condition must be reported by the employee to the 
employee’s immediate supervisor. All electrical equipment which does not require continuous 
operation should be switched off when not in use and plugs removed from socket outlets. Employees 
should never use equipment that is suspected to be faulty, or where the cable or flex is damaged or 
connections are loose. Any faults should be immediately reported to the employee’s immediate 
superior. 
 
b) Employees should not attempt to repair nor interfere with electrical equipment or do wiring by 
themselves; neither should they use dual or other socket outlets unless these have been properly 
authorized by an electrical engineer. 
 
Floors, Ladders, Steps and stairs: 
 
a) Cables and wires should not be permitted to trail across the floor where people may be passing, 
unless a covering is provided.  
 
b) Employees should take extra care on newly polished or wet floors.   
 
c) Where ladders or steps are used to reach above normal height, employees should ensure that they 
are firmly based and secure. Where necessary, assistance should be sought to prevent them 
slipping. Employees should exercise care on stairs and handrails should be used. 
 
Accidents or illnesses at work 
a)  Any accident or illness occurring on the work premises should be reported to the employees’ 
immediate supervisor, who should ensure that all details are entered in the accident report book. It 
is important that all injuries however slight should be reported.

b) Employees should ensure that they know the names and locations of the persons designated to take 
charge of the first aid cabinet and to render first aid in an emergency. In an emergency, speed is 
very crucial and an ambulance should be called in. 
 
General 
The attention of all employees is drawn to the safety Rules and Procedures as disciplinary action will be 
taken against any such employee who violates these Rules and Procedures. 
 
The Authority will consult with the employees periodically to ascertain what measures should be taken 
to increase awareness of health and safety and to ensure that all necessary measures are taken to make 
this policy effective. 
 
The Authority will take such measures as may be necessary to ensure proper training, and supervision 
and instructions of all employees in matters pertaining to their health and safety and to provide any 
necessary information.

SCHEDULE 9: SEXUAL HARASSMENT POLICY 
 
It is the Authority’s policy that all employees are responsible for assuring that the work place is free from 
sexual harassment. The Authority does not condone or excuse sexual harassment of any kind. Sexual 
harassment by management and supervisory staff, co-workers, non-employees over whom the Authority 
exercises some measure of control will not be tolerated on or off work premises.  
Because of the Authority’s strong disapproval of offensive or inappropriate sexual behavior with respect to 
the work place, all employees must avoid any action or conduct, which could be viewed as sexual 
harassment. Therefore, under this policy, the following acts are specifically prohibited: 
• Granting or denying job benefits based on receptivity of sexual advances, threatening or insinuating 
either expressly or implying that an employee’s refusal to submit to sex would deny him /her a job. 
•  Sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and verbal or physical conduct of a sexual harassing 
nature; 
• Intimate relationships between supervisory and subordinate employees on or off work premises, 
which may affect the supervisors’ treatment of the employee with respect to his or her terms and 
conditions of employment. 
• A working environment of unwelcome sexual conduct that has the purpose or effect of unreasonably 
interfering with an affected person’s work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile or offensive 
work environment: and 
• Sexual slurs, vulgar jokes, display of sexually explicit pictures, or other sexually offensive conduct. 
Any employee who has a complaint of sexual harassment with respect to the workplace by anyone including 
supervisors, co-workers, visitors, contractors, customers or any other person over whom the Authority 
exercises some measure of control should immediately bring the problem to the attention of the Director 
Human Resources. 
All complaints will be promptly handled on a confidential basis. The DHRA will retain confidential 
documentation of all allegations and investigations and will take appropriate corrective action to remedy all 
violations of this policy. 
Corrective action may include disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or termination 
of the business relationship for persons found to have engaged in sexual harassment.
